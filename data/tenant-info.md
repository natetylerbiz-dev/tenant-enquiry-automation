# Prospective & Current Tenant Information

## Payments & Property Management

There are two lease structures, and which one applies depends on the specific property (see property-specific information for this property's lease type):

- **Managed (RM) lease**: Harrow & Vale Properties manages the lease on the Landlord's behalf. Rent is paid into the agency's trust account, the agency issues monthly invoices, holds the deposit in trust, and is the point of contact for maintenance requests.
- **Tenant Placement Only (OF) lease**: Harrow & Vale Properties only introduces the tenant; the Landlord manages the lease directly from there — rent is paid to the Landlord, the Landlord issues invoices, holds the deposit, and is the point of contact for maintenance requests.

If the lease type for a property isn't known, don't guess who to pay or contact — say so and offer to check.

## Application Process

- Applications go through a background/credit check; by applying, tenants consent to the Landlord/agency checking with credit bureaus and to POPIA-compliant processing of personal information for this purpose.
- Providing false or incomplete information during the application is grounds for the Landlord to terminate the lease immediately if discovered later.

## Viewing Process

- Viewings are arranged via WhatsApp after an initial enquiry.
- A missed/no-show viewing appointment costs R500.
- Even during an active tenancy, if the property is going back on the market (near lease end, or being sold), the tenant must allow reasonable access for viewings by prospective tenants/buyers and allow marketing photos.

## Lease Terms & Renewal

- Exact rent and deposit amounts are set per property — see property-specific information for this property's figures. Don't guess these numbers.
- The standard initial lease period is 12 months (1 year). A request for a longer or shorter lease term is not something to confirm, estimate, or promise — it needs to go to the agent directly.
- Renewal: the Landlord/agency notifies the tenant of the upcoming expiry 30-60 business days before the lease ends. The tenant can accept a renewal (confirming in writing at least 15 business days before the lease ends) or give notice they don't want to continue (also at least 15 business days' notice). If neither happens, the lease automatically continues month-to-month, requiring 1 month's notice from either side to end it after that point.
- Tenants can't renew if they owe money to the Landlord at the time.

## Deposit & Fees

- The deposit is held in an interest-bearing account (interest to the tenant's benefit) for the duration of the lease — held by the Landlord for OF leases, or in the agency's trust account for RM leases (RM leases carry an annual R75 + VAT deposit account administration fee, deducted from the deposit).
- An administration fee applies to every lease: 8% of the monthly rental (+ VAT), capped at R850 + VAT, paid by the tenant to the agency. The exact rand amount depends on this property's rent — see property-specific information.
- The Landlord can deduct from the deposit for unpaid rent/utilities, damage beyond fair wear and tear, cleaning, or lost keys/remotes — if that happens, the tenant must top the deposit back up to the required amount.
- The remaining deposit plus interest is refunded within the timeframe set by the Rental Housing Act after the lease ends, once any deductions are settled.

## Cancelling Early (Tenant-Initiated)

- Individual tenants: at least 15 business days' written notice, and you remain liable for any arrears. Expect a cancellation penalty of about 2 months' rent if more than 6 months remain on the lease, or about 1 month's rent if less than 6 months remain — this can be reduced if a replacement tenant is found quickly on the same or better terms.
- Business/company tenants: at least 2 months' written notice, plus liability for arrears and rent until a new tenant is found.
- If you were directly marketed to and are cancelling within 5 business days of signing, you can cancel without penalty and get a full refund of any payments made.

## Landlord-Initiated Cancellation

- The Landlord can end the lease early on 2 months' notice only if they intend to move in themselves, or have sold/are selling the property.
- Repeated breaches (3 times) can lead to a 15-business-day termination notice.
- Illegal activity on the premises can result in immediate termination and a demand to vacate within 24 hours.

## Late Payment & Breach

- Interest of 2% per month applies to anything not paid on time.
- Breaches generally need to be fixed within 15 business days (7 calendar days for business/company tenants) of written notice before further action is taken.

## Pets

- Pets require the Landlord's prior written consent, and are also subject to any applicable municipal by-laws and Body Corporate/Sectional Title rules.

## Utilities

- The tenant pays for all utilities/services consumed (electricity, water, etc.), plus any alarm, telephone, internet, or DSTV/TV subscriptions.
- Municipal rates and Body Corporate levies are the Landlord's responsibility, not the tenant's.
- If a utility account is in the tenant's name and goes unpaid, the Landlord/agency can pay it from the deposit, and the tenant must then top the deposit back up.

## Maintenance & Repairs

- Structural items (exterior walls, roof, plumbing, electrical, other structural parts) are the Landlord's responsibility to repair, unless the tenant caused the damage.
- The tenant is responsible for interior upkeep — cleaning, maintaining fixtures/fittings, doors, locks, windows, light bulbs, taps, extractor fans — and must report any structural defect or damage in writing immediately.
- If there's a pool, the tenant keeps it clean and maintained (chemicals, filter, motor) at their own cost, but isn't liable for repairs/replacement of the pool structure or equipment unless they caused the damage.
- Report maintenance issues to whoever manages the lease for that property (see Payments & Property Management above).

## Subletting

## Move-In / Move-Out

- **Move-in**: a joint inspection happens before the tenant moves in, with any existing damage noted in an inspection report. The tenant then has 5 business days after the lease starts to report any additional defects in writing — anything not reported by then is assumed to not have existed.
- **Move-out**: the tenant must vacate by 12:00 on the final day, return all keys/remotes, and leave the property clean (including windows, cupboards, countertops, floors, doors, and inside the oven/stove) — professional cleaning will be charged to the tenant if it's not done to a satisfactory standard. A joint exit inspection happens; damage beyond fair wear and tear is deducted from the deposit.

## Liability & Insurance

- The tenant's own belongings are entirely at their own risk — the Landlord isn't responsible for insuring tenant possessions, and isn't liable for loss/damage from fire or burglary.
- If more than one person signs as tenant, they are jointly and severally liable — if one tenant doesn't pay, the others can be held responsible for the full amount, not just their share.
